The Evolution of Luxury

From horseless carriages to technological marvels, luxury automobiles have always defined the cutting edge

1906 - Rolls-Royce Silver Ghost

Dubbed "The Best Car in the World," it set new standards for quietness and smoothness with its 6-cylinder engine.

1930 - Duesenberg Model J

The American luxury car that rivaled European marques, offering custom coachwork and unprecedented power.

1955 - Mercedes-Benz 300SL Gullwing

With its iconic upward-opening doors and fuel-injected engine, it became the sports car for the elite.

1963 - Rolls-Royce Silver Cloud III

Featured a redesigned grille and improved V8 engine, becoming the preferred transport for royalty.

1987 - BMW 7 Series (E32)

Introduced revolutionary features like double glazing, electronic damping control, and on-board computer.

2003 - Maybach 62

Mercedes-Benz's ultra-luxury revival with rear seats that reclined to 47 degrees and optional perfume atomizers.
